Rice

Steps

  • Rinse the rice: Place the rice in a fine-mesh sieve or colander and rinse it under cold water. Swish the rice around with your fingers while rinsing to remove any excess starch.
  • Combine rice and water: In a medium-sized saucepan, combine the rinsed rice, water, and salt (if using). Stir briefly to mix everything together.
  • Bring to a boil: Place the saucepan over medium-high heat and bring the rice mixture to a boil. Stir once, then reduce the heat to low.
  • Simmer: Cover the saucepan with a tight-fitting lid and let the rice simmer on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, or until all the water is absorbed and the rice is tender. Avoid lifting the lid or stirring the rice while it's cooking.
  • Fluff and let it rest: Once the rice is cooked, remove the saucepan from the heat and let it sit, covered, for about 5 minutes. This resting time helps to separate the grains and allows any excess moisture to be absorbed.
  • Fluff with a fork: After the resting time, remove the lid and use a fork to gently fluff the rice, separating the grains. This will help to release any steam and prevent the rice from becoming sticky.
  • Serve: The rice is now ready to be served as a side dish or as a base for other recipes. Enjoy!
